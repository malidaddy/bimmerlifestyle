When this 2004 BMW E46 M3 rolled into our workshop, it had 180,000 kilometres on the clock and years of deferred maintenance showing. The owner's brief was simple: bring it back to life without losing its soul. Here is how we did it.

## The Starting Point

The car arrived with a laundry list of issues:

- **VANOS rattle** on cold starts
- **Rod bearing wear** confirmed via oil analysis
- **Worn suspension bushings** throughout
- **Faded and swirled paint** from years of improper washing
- **Cracked dashboard** and worn interior trim

Despite all of this, the chassis was solid and the S54 engine — one of BMW's greatest inline-six designs — still pulled strong above 4,000 RPM. The bones were worth saving.

## Engine Work

We started where it matters most. The S54 came out for a full refresh:

- **Rod bearing replacement** with ACL race bearings
- **VANOS rebuild** with new seals and updated solenoids
- **Valve adjustment** to factory specification
- **New water pump, thermostat, and expansion tank** (the N-series cooling system weak points)
- **Fresh fluids** throughout — engine oil, transmission, differential

With the engine back in and broken in properly, the S54 was pulling cleanly to its 8,000 RPM redline.

## Suspension and Brakes

Next, we addressed the handling:

- **KW V3 coilovers** replacing the tired OEM shocks and springs
- **Fresh control arm bushings** and ball joints front and rear
- **StopTech slotted rotors** with EBC Yellowstuff pads
- **New wheel alignment** dialed in for spirited street driving

The transformation in turn-in response was immediately noticeable on the test drive.

## The Result

After four weeks of work, the E46 M3 was returned to its owner — driving better than it did when it left the factory. The S54 breathes freely, the chassis is tight, and the brakes inspire confidence at every corner.
